AND BUT SO WHAT'S
THIS
?
David Foster Wallace’s
Infinite
Jest
(1996) is one of the most rewarding novels ever written,
but it’s an admittedly tough sell. The sheer size of the
thing is more than enough to scare off many potential readers.
Then there are the issues of, you know, a dauntingly complex plot,
intricate symbolism, maddening postmodern ambiguity, lots of really
big words, and less-than-entirely-cheery subject matter. Convincing
a novice that all of this is
fun
is a job in itself.
The last thing a reader needs to worry
about is keeping track of the book’s characters. Show me
a person who can follow all 200+ in
Infinite Jest
(
and
their initials and variants) and I’ll show you DFW with a
crib sheet. Herewith is a guide to keeping all those tennis prodigies,
drug addicts, and radical Quebecois separatists straight. For
both initiates and experienced endnote-junkies, this guide is
intended to provide support to anyone wondering who’s who
and what bearing they all have on the story, with brief character
summaries, related critical plot points, and occasional commentary
/ speculation from yours truly.
Characters are arranged alphabetically
by last name (unless not applicable, e.g. "Sixties Bob"
or "Sergeant-at-Arms").
Because of the impossibilty
of discussing the characters without giving away plot points,
I've framed even remotely sensitive information between asterisks
(
***
Like this--wow nifty
***
). To reveal the text, highlight
the blank space between the asterisks (left click and drag the
pointer over the space).
By no means
do the entries spill all of the novel's secrets, but for bewildered
readers it should at least point in the right direction.
Have fun with it, and please e-mail
me with any corrections, characters I’ve missed (should I
include the puppet show?), theories on the novel’s mysteries,
and recommendations of cool music and other stuff.
Most material probably copyright
(c) David Foster Wallace. Everything else copyright (c) various
organizations and corporations. Please don’t sue me; I have
student loans to pay.
